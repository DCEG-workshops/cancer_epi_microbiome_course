## Welcome

This short course provides an overview of the process of conducting studies of the human microbiome and cancer risk. We focus on studies of the bacterial component of the human microbiome and include discussion of the mycobiome (fungi).

### Course Format

- **9 sessions**, each approximately 90 minutes
- Lectures with ample time for questions and discussion
- Hands-on practical sessions for data analysis (Sessions 5, 6, 7)
- Mix of in-person and virtual attendance options

### Course Topics

The course covers the complete pipeline from sample collection to statistical analysis:

1. **Overview of the Human Microbiome** - Introduction, H. pylori story, and ecological concepts *(Abnet, MEB)*
2. **Sample Collection, Storage, and QC** - Best practices for oral and fecal samples *(Sinha, MEB)*
3. **DNA Extraction & Sequencing** - Laboratory quality control systems *(Dagnall & Jones, CGR)*
4. **QIIME 2 for Bioinformatics** - Data processing and analysis *(Greg Caporaso, Northern Arizona University)*
5. **Statistical Analysis** - Methods for microbiome data *(Shi & Anyaso Samuel, BB)*
6. **Meta-analysis & Data Sharing** - Integration and repositories *(Herrera, MEB & Wan, CGR)*
7. **Hands-on Data Analysis (R)** - Practical R workshop *(Yano & Ferro, MEB)*
8. **Tissue Microbiome Studies** - Special considerations using Sherlock Lung *(McElderry, ITEB)*
9. **Summary & Special Considerations** - Study design and epidemiological considerations *(Vogtmann, MEB)*
